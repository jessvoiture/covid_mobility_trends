Top Down or Bottom Up
Voluntary versus governmental responses to COVID-19 and their effect on mobility trends
Google’s mobility trends track how visits and length of stay at different places change compared to a baseline period (January 3, 2020 to February 6, 2020) and thus provide insight into some of the ways human behavior and travel patterns changed as a result of the pandemic.
As the scale and geographic reach of the pandemic swelled in the early months of 2020, mobility and travel tandemly contracted. Mobility to workplaces, retail and recreational places, and transit are all observed drastic reductions as stores closed and people shifted to online school and work.
Effect of Rising Covid Cases on Mobility
Weekly new covid cases and decrease in mobility from baseline for the first wave of Covid
As the early months of the pandemic unfolded communities and individuals may have responded to the global trend of the pandemic by self-regulating their mobility—far outpacing health guidelines or policy restrictions. Unlike many countries (and in true American fashion), the US did not impose a nation-wide lockdown. Instead, stay-at-home orders and other health measures were at the discretion of state governors, leading to a mosaic of mobility restrictions and variation in when these policies were imposed. The devolution of public health decisions engendered a partisan divide in responses, with many states headed by Republican leadership resisting stringent policies and comprehensive lockdowns.
